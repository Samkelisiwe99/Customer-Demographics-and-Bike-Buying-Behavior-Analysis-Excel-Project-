# Customer-Demographics-and-Bike-Buying-Behavior-Analysis-Excel-Project-
This Excel project uncovers how factors like age, income, education, and marital status shape customer decisions to buy bikes. Through data cleaning, pivot tables, and dashboards, it reveals how lifestyle and purchasing power influence consumer behavior.

## üîç Key Insights
1. **Marital Status and Income:**  
   Married individuals earn higher average incomes than single individuals, resulting in greater purchasing power. Married people (both male and female) are more likely to buy bikes.

2. **Gender and Purchasing Power:**  
   Among females, single women tend to have lower income and purchasing power, leading to fewer bike purchases compared to married women.

3. **Car Ownership and Bike Purchases:**  
   - People with **no cars** purchase the fewest bikes ‚Äî likely due to lower income levels.  
   - Those with **one car** are the most likely to purchase a bike.  
   - Individuals with **multiple cars** are less interested in buying bikes, possibly due to higher car dependency.

4. **Education Level:**  
   Customers with **lower educational qualifications (e.g., high school diploma)** tend to purchase more bikes than those with higher degrees.

5. **Age Group:**  
   - **Middle-aged (30‚Äì49)** and **older (50+)** individuals are the main bike buyers.  
   - **Younger customers (<30)** are the least likely to purchase bikes.

6. **Commute Distance:**  
   People who live **closer to work** are more likely to purchase bikes, while those with **longer commutes** are less likely.
